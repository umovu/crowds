Two modes

A wind tunnel for words and offers.

Same engine, two lenses. Pick the one that matches what you're about to ship.

Policy wind tunnel

Citizens reacting to an announcement.

Pressure-test policy drafts, press releases, and crisis comms against a synthetic public before they meet the real one.

  • Red-team a policy — surface likely objections, blind spots, and ways a rule gets gamed before you commit.
  • Test framing — see how different groups read or misread your wording.
  • Rehearse crisis comms — pressure-test an announcement and the reactions you'd need to handle.
Try policy mode →
Product wind tunnel

A market reacting to an idea.

Stress-test a product pitch against a South African market — qualitative willingness and adoption barriers, not purchase predictions.

  • Stress-test product ideas — see objections, confusion, and qualitative willingness (not purchase forecasts).
  • Identify market segments — understand which customer types are receptive vs. resistant.
  • Uncover adoption barriers — real-world constraints like price sensitivity, data costs, and trust.

What makes it a sandbox

Don't just watch it. Step into it.

A passive demo shows you reactions. A sandbox lets you change the inputs and see what moves.

⏸

Intervene mid-run

Pause a live simulation, pose a follow-up — "we'll add a subsidy" — and watch how opinions shift in the next round.

◔

Probe after the fact

Read each agent's expressed opinion post-run, then pose interventions and see how specific personas respond.

＋

Bring your own people

Inject custom agents — named stakeholders, specific groups — merged into the population, or run on their own.

Read this before you rely on it

Coverage, not prediction. Personas, not people.

What it is
A broad survey of the kinds of responses your content might draw — directional signal to prepare against.
What it isn't
A forecast, a poll, or a statistically representative sample of public opinion.
What it is
Personas grounded in real socio-economic context, so reactions are plausible.
What it isn't
A claim that any agent mirrors a real individual. Grounding makes them plausible, not accurate.

What it isn't
